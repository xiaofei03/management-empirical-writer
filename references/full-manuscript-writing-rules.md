# Full Manuscript Writing Rules

This is the primary writing authority for empirical management manuscripts. Read it before planning, drafting, revising, translating, or reviewing any substantive manuscript section.

Apply these rules by section function rather than fixed chapter number. A target journal may combine, split, rename, or reorder sections, but the argument must retain the complete chain below.

If another reference in this skill provides more detailed section guidance, use it only after this file and interpret it consistently with this file. If rules conflict, this file governs manuscript rhetoric and cross-section allocation; evidence-integrity, citation-field, and formal-delivery safety gates remain absolute.

## 1. Governing Argument Chain

Build one continuous argument:

```text
important phenomenon and tension
-> unresolved research question
-> theoretical lens and mechanism
-> testable predictions
-> reproducible empirical design
-> evidence on whether predictions hold
-> evidence on why and when they hold
-> precise change to theory and practice
```

Assign one primary function to each manuscript section:

| Section function | Governing question |
|---|---|
| Introduction | Why does this problem matter, and what remains unresolved? |
| Literature, Theory, and Hypotheses | Why should the proposed relationships hold? |
| Research Design | How are constructs measured and hypotheses tested? |
| Empirical Results | Do the core predictions receive empirical support? |
| Further Analyses | Why, when, and under which boundaries do relationships occur? |
| Discussion and Conclusion | What do the findings change in theory and practice? |

Do not optimize sections independently. A strong paragraph is still misplaced if it answers another section's governing question.

## 2. Manuscript-Wide Preconditions

Before body drafting:

1. Confirm the research question, theoretical lens, construct roles, model, sample, and target-journal profile.
2. Confirm the alignment report and writing plan.
3. Build the literature pool, citation plan, empirical evidence map, and variable dictionary.
4. Confirm writing-critical literature has full-text support and valid citekeys where required.
5. Inventory every empirical table, figure, output, and user summary before empirical sections.
6. Map each planned substantive claim to literature, an Evidence ID, an inventoried result artifact, or explicit user confirmation.

Do not draft around missing evidence, fabricate a clean story, hide unsupported hypotheses, or use rhetoric to exceed the identification design.

Before revising an existing manuscript, diagnose the full chain rather than editing sentences in isolation:

- locate cross-section repetition;
- locate broken transitions between question, theory, design, results, and contribution;
- locate claims without evidence;
- locate evidence without a clear argumentative role;
- locate mechanisms or boundary conditions that appear only after results;
- locate contributions that are not earned by the preceding argument.

## 3. Introduction

### 3.1 Required Logic

Build the introduction in this order:

```text
focal phenomenon
-> tension or contradiction
-> themed synthesis of prior research
-> specific unresolved gap
-> research question
-> theoretical lens
-> data and findings preview
-> precise contributions
```

Open with the concrete phenomenon and focal tension. Avoid macro-era slogans, broad policy lists, early theory definitions, and previews of every mechanism or moderator.

Synthesize literature by concept, antecedent, consequence, mechanism, or boundary condition. Do not write an author-by-author catalogue. State the gap as a specific theoretical or empirical blind spot tied directly to the research question, not merely `few studies have examined`.

Explain why the outcome matters through its organizational foundations and show why the focal explanatory variable could affect those foundations.

Introduce the theory after the problem is clear. State why it explains the focal tension better than plausible alternatives and preview how it connects the independent variable, mechanisms, moderators, and outcome.

Preview the data, sample, focal measure, main findings, and contributions briefly. Do not perform the full theory derivation or results discussion in the introduction.

### 3.2 Introduction Gate

Confirm that a reader can answer:

- What important phenomenon creates the research problem?
- What exact tension or unresolved relationship motivates the study?
- Why does the outcome matter?
- Why is the selected theory suitable?
- What evidence will test the question?
- Which precise understanding may change?

## 4. Literature Review, Theory, and Hypotheses

### 4.1 Theory Architecture

Do not repeat the introduction's background. Move from theory to variable mapping to mechanism to prediction:

```text
theory source and original problem
-> core theoretical mechanism
-> management application
-> mapping to this setting and constructs
-> hypothesis logic
```

Identify the theoretical actor, behavior or signal, receiver, unobservable quality, mechanism, feedback or verification process, and boundary condition. For signaling-style arguments, also identify signal cost and credibility.

Do not begin hypothesis writing until each mediator represents a named theoretical process and each moderator changes a specific link in that process.

### 4.2 Hypothesis Derivation

Use this sequence for every hypothesis:

```text
theoretical basis
-> operating mechanism
-> expected direction
-> competing force or boundary when relevant
-> formal hypothesis
```

Do not start from an observed coefficient and search backward for a theory.

For nonlinear hypotheses, explain why benefits exceed costs initially, what changes at the turning stage, why costs later exceed benefits, and what makes the marginal effect change sign.

For mediation, explain how X changes a theory-defined process, why the mediator captures that process, and why the process changes Y. Do not treat `X -> M -> Y` as sufficient theory.

For moderation, identify whether the moderator changes information acquisition, interpretation, verification, resource response, behavioral constraint, or another theory-defined link. Do not merely predict a stronger or weaker coefficient.

### 4.3 Citation Discipline

Give every citation an argumentative role: concept definition, theory support, mechanism support, gap establishment, measurement validation, or boundary-condition justification.

Use 1-3 references in a normal citation group. Require a clear shared function for 4-5 references. Split groups above 5 by claim function unless the user approves a rare exception. Preserve foundational theory, method, and measurement sources even when they are older.

## 5. Research Design

### 5.1 Required Logic

Answer where the data come from, how variables are measured, and how hypotheses are tested. Do not repeat motivation, theory, contribution, or implications.

Build a reproducible sample path:

```text
initial sample
-> source matching
-> exclusions
-> missing-data treatment
-> outlier treatment
-> final estimation sample
```

Report the setting, period, unit of analysis, source-variable mapping, matching keys, timing alignment, exclusions, and exact winsorization or trimming rules. Explain sample loss caused by lags, missing variables, or fixed effects when relevant. Check look-ahead bias.

For every variable, state the construct, source, calculation or coding rule, indicator direction, and literature or confirmed source. Keep core-variable construction sufficiently detailed to reproduce; move dictionaries, parameter lists, and auxiliary diagnostics to an appendix when appropriate.

Distinguish original measurement development from adaptation, refinement, application, or improved operationalization. Create a separate measurement-validation subsection only when genuine validation evidence exists.

Keep controls concise and check that they are not mediators, outcome components, post-treatment variables, or significance-driven additions.

For each model, state the dependent variable, focal terms, controls, fixed effects, standard-error estimator, and clustering level.

For nonlinear models, specify coefficient directions, turning-point calculation, effective sample range, and formal shape test. For mediation, distinguish causal mediation from mechanism-consistent evidence. For moderation, state the affected theoretical link, centering, interaction construction, and planned marginal-effect or simple-slope test.

### 5.2 Research Design Gate

Confirm that the sample and core variables can be reconstructed, time windows align, measurement claims are not overstated, and model choices match the hypotheses and data structure.

## 6. Empirical Results

### 6.1 Required Logic

Use descriptive statistics to establish variation, plausible ranges, and multicollinearity risk. Do not narrate every cell.

Write each substantive result paragraph as:

```text
finding
-> substantive magnitude or meaning
-> connection to the theoretical prediction
```

Do not read tables column by column or interpret every control. Report economic significance where possible through standard-deviation changes, interquartile shifts, marginal effects, predicted probabilities, or turning points.

For nonlinear findings, check the formal shape test, slopes at the low and high ends, turning point, confidence interval, effective data range, and a figure based on model predictions. Do not infer a U-shape from coefficient signs alone.

Use robustness tests to address plausible alternative explanations, not to increase table count. Explain the risk addressed and summarize the combined conclusion.

Distinguish robustness from identification. Lagged variables, PSM, added fixed effects, and alternative measures usually alleviate concerns; credible natural experiments, DID, RDD, instruments, or external shocks may justify stronger causal language after assumptions pass.

When binary PSM is used for a continuous treatment, report the threshold and rationale, covariates, method, caliper, replacement, ratio, balance, and common support.

### 6.2 Results Language Gate

Use `is associated with`, `provides evidence consistent with`, or `helps alleviate concerns` when strong causal identification is absent. Do not use `proves`, `establishes causality`, or `causal effect` beyond the design.

Report unsupported, mixed, and nonsignificant findings honestly. Never simplify the narrative by omitting planned evidence.

## 7. Further Analyses

### 7.1 Required Logic

Retain a mechanism, moderation, heterogeneity, or additional analysis only when it answers a named theoretical question.

For mechanisms:

```text
theoretical role of mediator
-> X-to-M evidence
-> M-to-Y or indirect-path evidence
-> theoretical interpretation
-> calibrated claim strength
```

Explain whether multiple mechanisms are complementary or competing and whether they represent distinct resource, cognition, governance, behavior, internal, or external processes.

For moderation, identify the changed theoretical link, report the interaction, show simple slopes, marginal effects, or regions of significance when appropriate, and interpret the resulting boundary condition.

Treat heterogeneity as boundary-condition analysis rather than routine subgroup regression. Prefer theoretically meaningful actor, resource, information, institutional, capability, or uncertainty dimensions. Use ownership, size, region, or age only when theory gives them a clear role.

Do not claim a group difference because one coefficient is significant and another is not. Use an appropriate coefficient-difference, bootstrap, interaction, seemingly unrelated estimation, or Chow test.

Label analyses without ex ante theory as exploratory, additional, or post hoc. Do not convert an unexpected result into an allegedly prior hypothesis or invent a new theory to rationalize it.

### 7.2 Further Analyses Gate

Confirm that mechanisms explain why, moderators explain when or through which link, boundary tests use formal differences, unsupported results remain visible, and no analysis exists only to add length.

## 8. Discussion and Conclusion

### 8.1 Theoretical Interpretation

Select two to four central findings and explain why they arise, how they relate to prior theory, whether they challenge linear, static, or single-mechanism assumptions, and what unsupported findings reveal about theoretical boundaries. Do not repeat coefficients or a hypothesis checklist.

For an unsupported result, state it clearly, distinguish theory, measurement, power, and design explanations, identify the possible boundary, and propose a future test without rewriting the current evidence.

### 8.2 Contributions

Build every contribution as:

```text
prior understanding
-> limitation
-> precise extension, qualification, or change
-> why the change matters
```

Retain the two or three strongest contributions. Do not substitute a findings list or vague statements such as `enriches the literature`, `broadens the perspective`, or `fills a gap`. Verify any `first` or `novel` claim against the literature.

### 8.3 Implications, Limitations, and Ending

For every practical implication, identify the actor, action, evidence-based rationale, and condition of applicability. Match recommendation strength to the sample and identification design; do not overextend into universal policy claims.

Frame limitations as researchable boundaries:

```text
what the study does
-> what remains unresolved
-> why the boundary matters
-> how future research can address it
```

Use one concise conclusion paragraph to state the focal phenomenon, central mechanism, and most important boundary. Do not relist hypotheses or introduce a new theory.

## 9. Cross-Section Integration

After every substantive chapter round, inspect adjacent sections:

- Introduction versus Theory: keep problem framing separate from full mechanism derivation.
- Theory versus Research Design: keep construct meaning separate from operationalization.
- Research Design versus Results: keep planned tests separate from findings.
- Results versus Further Analyses: keep core support separate from mechanisms and boundaries.
- Further Analyses versus Discussion: keep statistical evidence separate from theoretical advancement.
- Discussion versus Introduction: ensure claimed contributions answer the original gap and do not introduce a different paper.

Use transitions that carry forward the same research question, theory, constructs, and evidence. Do not let later sections silently change construct meaning, hypothesis direction, model interpretation, or contribution target.

## 10. Writing and Evidence Standards

Give each paragraph one primary job:

```text
topic sentence
-> evidence or reasoning
-> implication for the current section's governing question
```

Do not combine result reporting, literature review, implications, and limitations in one paragraph.

Prefer calibrated phrases such as `the results show`, `the findings indicate`, `the evidence is consistent with`, `this suggests`, `this extends`, `this qualifies`, and `this reveals a boundary condition`.

Keep every substantive statement traceable. When an Evidence ID has pending QA, use cautious wording and do not present the claim as settled.

Preserve bilingual equivalence in question, theory, structure, models, evidence, figures, tables, hypotheses, citations, and claims. Adapt rhetoric to language and journal without creating two substantively different papers.

## 11. Mandatory Full-Manuscript Audit

Do not mark a manuscript or chapter round complete until all applicable items pass:

- The manuscript forms one `problem -> theory -> design -> evidence -> explanation -> contribution` argument.
- Every section answers its governing question and avoids taking over another section's role.
- The original gap and final contributions correspond directly.
- Theory maps explicitly to constructs, mechanisms, and boundaries.
- Hypotheses derive from theory rather than observed coefficients.
- Sample, measurement, models, fixed effects, standard errors, and clustering are reproducible.
- Every empirical claim maps to verified evidence and every provided result artifact is accounted for.
- Economic significance, nonlinear shape, robustness purpose, and identification strength are reported accurately.
- Mechanism, moderation, and heterogeneity claims do not exceed their tests.
- Unsupported and exploratory findings are visible and labeled honestly.
- Contributions specify what prior understanding changes and why.
- Implications and limitations match evidence boundaries.
- Cross-section repetition is removed without deleting unique evidence.
- No data, result, citation, citekey, test, or theoretical justification is invented.
- Chinese and English manuscripts remain substantively synchronized under the active lifecycle mode.

If any item fails, revise the argument chain before polishing prose or formatting.

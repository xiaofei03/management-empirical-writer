# Bilingual Writing Rules

Use this reference whenever the skill maintains both Chinese and English drafts.

## Goal

Keep two publication-ready argument tracks aligned on facts while allowing language-specific rhetorical adaptation.

## Non-Negotiable Consistency Rules

The Chinese and English drafts must agree on:

- Research question
- Theoretical logic
- Hypothesis direction
- Data facts
- Sample description
- Variable meanings
- Model specification
- Fixed effects
- Clustering level
- Regression interpretation
- Table numbering
- Citation set

## What Must Differ

The two versions should differ in expression, pacing, and emphasis when appropriate.

### Chinese draft tendencies

Often give more space to:

- Real-world problem framing
- Policy relevance
- Management relevance
- Fuller transitions between theory and empirical context
- More explicit practical interpretation

### English draft tendencies

Often give more space to:

- Research gap
- Theoretical contribution
- Identification logic
- Robustness logic
- Tighter paragraphing and stronger contribution signaling

## Do Not Translate Literally

Avoid these failure modes:

- Translating every sentence from Chinese to English
- Translating every sentence from English to Chinese
- Carrying Chinese discourse markers directly into English
- Flattening English contribution framing into generic Chinese prose

## Recommended Workflow

1. Confirm shared evidence base.
2. Decide the purpose of the current chapter in each language.
3. Draft the Chinese version in a native Chinese academic style.
4. Draft the English version in a native SSCI or management-journal style.
5. Compare both versions against the consistency checklist.
6. Resolve mismatches immediately.

## Mismatch Checklist

Check for these common problems:

- A hypothesis appears in one language but not the other
- A coefficient is interpreted differently across languages
- A variable name is translated inconsistently
- Policy implications appear in Chinese without corresponding evidence in English
- The English draft claims theoretical contribution more strongly than the evidence supports

## Abstract Rule

Write the abstract last. The abstract must reflect the final aligned state of:

- research question
- design
- main findings
- contribution
- implications
